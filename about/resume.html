Confidential Federated Learning with Homomorphic Encryption Stockholm, Sweden
Master Thesis conducted in CanaryBit AB supervised by Dr. Nicolae Paladi and Prof. Roberto Guanciale 03/2023 - 09/2023
  • Developed Heflp, a homomorphic encryption–enhanced confidential federated learning framework supporting both PyTorch and TensorFlow as ML backbones. Integrated both classic HE schemes (CKKS, BFV) and a self-developed innovative FL-dedicated scheme Flashev2.
  • Designed a robust model aggregation mechanism MWAvg to mitigate risk of overflowing in large-scale client scenarios, maintaining efficiency without additional computational and memory overhead.
  • Evaluated the framework across diverse settings—varying model architectures, client counts, ML tasks, and HE schemes—to benchmark and compare performance.

ML-Stealer: Stealing Prediction Functionality of Machine Learning Models Wuhan, China
National Undergraduate Training Program for Innovation supervised by Prof. Chen Wang 04/2020-05/2021

  • Designed loss function Applying focal-loss strategy to relieve the effect of unevenly distributed training dataset.
  • Achieved an averaged accuracy of 85.6% and up to 93.6% at best under the mere black-box access scenario.
